The Story

Why I'm Building This

1990s
Where it started — Anantapur, India

Growing up in Anantapur district, the news was full of farmer suicides. Crops failing, families losing everything they'd grown. I was a child, but it stayed with me. I didn't have a word for it then — but I was already thinking about plants, food, survival.

2010
The first company — Penguin Biotech, Madurai

I almost started a mushroom production company. I don't even eat mushrooms. But that wasn't the point. The point was building — creating something beyond myself. The instinct to start was already there, looking for any outlet.

2015–24
The science — ten years of groundwork

PhD, then postdoc at SLU Alnarp. Plant viruses, RNA biology, crop protection. In 2021, we published the first demonstration of RNA-based protection against Phytophthora infestans — the pathogen behind the Irish Potato Famine, still destroying billions in crops today. The science that would become a company was being built, paper by paper.

Mar 2025
The decision — stop writing about it, start solving it

A friend reminded me recently: "Twenty years ago you were already talking about plant biology — and you haven't stopped." In early 2025, I co-founded a pre-seed biotech startup. Not because the timing was perfect. Because after a decade of publishing, I wanted results that didn't sit on a shelf.

Now
Building in stealth — here's what I can share

Post-harvest crop losses cost the global supply chain billions every year. We're working on a biology-based solution — precise, sustainable, no chemical residues. What started in a greenhouse in Alnarp is now a company with a team, real commercial traction, and a very long runway of conviction behind it.
