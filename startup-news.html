The story

Why I'm building this, in my own words.

“Twenty years ago you were already talking about plant biology, and you haven't stopped.”A friend, on a throughline I never set out to follow.
1990s · Anantapur, India

Where it started.

Growing up in a drought-prone district, the news was full of failing crops and families losing everything they had grown. I was a child, but it stayed with me. I didn't have the words for it then, but I was already thinking about plants, food, and survival.

2010 · India

My first company.

I started a small agricultural production company. The science and the execution were sound, but it never took off. That taught me what I carry into everything since: a company stands or falls on its business and commercial side, not on technical merit alone.

2015 – 2024 · Sweden

Ten years of groundwork.

A PhD and then a postdoc in plant viruses, RNA biology, and crop protection. Along the way, the first demonstration of RNA-based protection against the pathogen behind the Irish Potato Famine, which still destroys billions in crops today. The science that would become a company was being built, paper by paper.

Early 2025

Stop writing about it.
Start solving it.

After a decade of publishing, I wanted results that did not sit on a shelf. So I co-founded an early-stage venture, not because the timing was perfect, but because the science was ready and the problem would not wait.

Now · Building in stealth

Here's what I can share.

Crops are lost between harvest and use at a scale that costs the global supply chain billions every year. We are working on a biology-based answer that is precise, sustainable, and leaves no chemical residue. What began as applied research is now a venture with a team, signed early partners, and real-world validation behind it.

Where it stands

From concept toward the field.

i.

The science is proven

The core method is published in peer-reviewed journals, reproduced, and understood. The company did not start from a hunch.

ii.

It works outside the lab

The approach has moved from a research concept to controlled real-world testing, with results that hold up beyond a single bench.

iii.

A team and real demand

The company started with two people and now has a small, capable team, a clear technical plan, an intellectual-property roadmap, and signed contracts with the people who will use the product.

This stretch: refining the core technical question, processing another round of greenhouse data, and the first proper conversations with the people who would eventually rely on the product. More on each as they develop.
