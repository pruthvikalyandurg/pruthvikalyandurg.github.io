🟢 Published · Confocal Fluorescence
Cyprosin B Subcellular Localisation

Confocal fluorescence microscopy of Nicotiana benthamiana leaf epidermal cells expressing GFP-tagged Cyprosin B variants. Reveals vacuolar targeting and endocytic vesicle localisation mediated by the Plant-Specific Insert (PSI) domain — with direct implications for plant-based protein production systems.

Muthusamy S, Kalyandurg PB et al., Plant Biotechnol. J. 2025 ↗

Fluorescence confocal microscopy imaging of plant cells during host-pathogen interaction. This type of imaging reveals how pathogens reorganise plant cell architecture — including plasmodesmata, vacuoles, and microtubule networks — to facilitate infection.

How to Read These Images

Not a microscopist? Here is a quick guide to what you are seeing.

Technique
Confocal Fluorescence Microscopy

Uses laser light to excite fluorescent tags attached to specific proteins. Different colours = different proteins. Allows 3D imaging of single cells with extraordinary resolution. Used to track where proteins go inside cells.

What to look for
Colour Channels

Green often indicates GFP (Green Fluorescent Protein) tagging. Red/magenta can mark membranes, nuclei, or separate proteins. Overlapping colours (yellow) show where two proteins co-localise — often meaning they interact.

Why it matters
Seeing the Invisible

Cells are ~10–50 micrometres across — invisible to the naked eye. Microscopy turns these invisible battles between plant and pathogen into something we can see, measure, and understand — driving the science behind sustainable crop protection.
